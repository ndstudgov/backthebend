Robinson Community Learning Center

Hosted by Robinson Community Learning Center

Location: 921 N Eddy St, South Bend, IN 46617

Number of Volunteers Needed: 15

The Robinson Community Learning Center (RCLC) was started in 2001 as an off-campus educational initiative of the President’s Office at the University of Notre Dame in partnership with Northeast Neighborhood residents of South Bend.

The RCLC offers a wide range of educational programs, including the Youth Development Afterschool Program, the Take Ten conflict resolution education program, Youth Entrepreneurship, LegoRobotics, Shakespeare, Literacy Tutoring, activities for Senior Adults, English As A New Language (ENL) classes for adults, as well as an ENL Preschool and the Talk With Your Baby program. An estimated 500 participants come through the doors of the Robinson Community Learning Center each week for regular programming. Participants range in age from 6 weeks to 96 years, and the population served is racially and ethnically diverse. RCLC programs are implemented with the assistance of 500 student volunteers from Notre Dame and other local institutions of higher learning.

The RCLC Murals were first painted more than 15 years ago by youth participants working with a professional artist. The mural panels, which represent neighborhood scenes as well as images of “heroes,” have become the RCLC’s “brand.” Ask anyone in the neighborhood about the Robinson Center and they will say “that’s the building with the murals!” Unfortunately, all of the mural panels are plywood, which deteriorates after about three years in the Indiana weather. Volunteers on Back the Bend Day paint new panels that recreate the original designs and will replace those panels that have aged.
